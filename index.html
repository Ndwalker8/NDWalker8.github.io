About Me

Welcome to my Aerospace Engineering Portfolio. My name is Nicholas Walker, and I'm a Senior Aerospace and Mechanical Engineer with a strong background in systems, project management, and design.

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI), with an expected graduation in May 2025. Alongside my major, I have also pursued a minor in Economics, allowing me to approach engineering challenges with a broader perspective.

Throughout my academic career, I have gained hands-on experience in various fields of aerospace and mechanical engineering, particularly through my roles as a Steampath Design Intern at GE Vernova, where I contributed to full-scale steam turbine bucket layout simulations. Additionally, I have conducted research on structural damage diagnostics as an undergraduate researcher at RPI, focusing on FEM/SEM damage diagnostic techniques.

I'm driven by a deep interest in innovation and technology, which is evident in my projects ranging from the design and analysis of vertical fin arrays for heat sinks to the development of UAV structural diagnostics. My technical skills span across various tools and software, including CATIA, ANSYS, Siemens NX, MATLAB, Python, and more

Thank you for taking the time to explore my portfolio. This is just a glimpse of my journey so far, and I'm excited to continue pushing the boundaries of what's possible in aerospace and mechanical engineering.
